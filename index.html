History of Deportation in the United States

An interactive timeline exploring the evolution of US immigration enforcement and deportation policy from the colonial era to the present

Adapted from: "History of Deportation," American Historical Association Congressional Briefing (March 2025), featuring historians Hidetaka Hirota (UC Berkeley), Ana Raquel Minian (Stanford), and Yael Schacher (Refugees International). View original resource →

This timeline is designed as a teaching tool for students learning about US immigration history. Each entry includes historical context and research questions to encourage inquiry-based learning. Primary source links connect students to digitized archival materials, legislation, court cases, and historical documents.

Created for educational use. For questions about sources or pedagogical approaches, consult with your instructor or librarian.
